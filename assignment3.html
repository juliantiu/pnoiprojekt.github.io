First of, it's good to finally see someone who had the same reason that I did for taking this class. Kristopher and I both want another A in our transcipt.

The other things that the current CSCE 445 students have in common with the '04 CSCS 445 students include phone usage, instant messaging , internet usage, email usage , and even someone ahead of the game who already started using online music services.

Yes. Even though these were students 15 years ago, people didn't necessarily change as much as devices. Whether it is through watching televsion or listening to music or talking to friends, people will always seek sources of entertainment and socialization. This is the same now, 15 years ago, or 50 years ago. Only the medium of media changed.

Boy, do we use a lot of different media usage compared to students 15 years ago. Among these things include actual newspapers, landline telephones, the radio, mp3 players, car stereos, old television shows, and paper books!

Furthermore, just naming a few, they did not have Youtube, Netflix, Spotify, Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, Snapchat, and they actually studied because they didn't have Chegg.

Now I'm not saying that we no longer watch television or listen to the radio today, but these are by no means the main medium in which we watch our shows or listen to our music. However, devices such as the landline telephone and mp3 players that were so popular back then are rare gems nowadays. Although I am glad to see that no one from '04 put walkman. They would be calssified as '04's hipsters.

Such a drastic change even only from 15 years ago is due to the advance in recent technologies. The devices that we have today must accomodate for the trend of rapid exchange of information. Many devices developed today are made with the consideration of how it can transfer data in the most efficient way. Changes in the technology we had back then versus what we have now may also be due to the convenience of miniaturization and portability. Phones, laptops, and telvisions are no longer seperate devices. This leads to the obsoleteness of some devices, because why have three different devices when you can have one in your pocket? This also paved the way for "smart" devices and the internet of things.
